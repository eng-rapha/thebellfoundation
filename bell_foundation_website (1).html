Our History

A legacy of compassion
rooted in Uganda's story

Founded
1989

Born out of the desperation witnessed during and after the Luwero Triangle war, BELL Foundation began as community help activities, formally registered as a CBO in 1991 and as a registered charity in 2024.

Named after Sir Henry Hesketh Joudou Bell (1864–1952) — Uganda's first official Governor — the Foundation is a tribute by Christopher Paul, his grandson, to a legacy of community transformation. Sir Hesketh's innovations transformed Uganda into a self-reliant agricultural powerhouse by 1914.

Starting in 1991 as grassroots community help activities, BELL Foundation for the Less Privileged has worked to deal with pain and suffering in a manner that respects those affected — with dignity, care, and time-consciousness.

The driving force was witnessing the misery of the people in the Luwero triangle. Christopher Paul, with roots in Uganda, chose to make a tangible change — carrying forward his grandfather's spirit of service and transformation.

Today, the Foundation continues to transform the lives of disadvantaged young people and communities in Central Uganda and parts of the north and Karamoja regions through community health, education, productive skilling, disaster action, and economic empowerment.

We operate on the conviction that poverty is man-made and can be addressed through positive human action — and that pain and suffering cross all borders, religions, and political divides.

Our work is grounded in a clear set of principles that guide every decision and every programme we deliver.

Vision

"A society where the less privileged thrive in dignity, empowered to build sustainable lives and resilient communities."

Mission

"To empower marginalized communities through education, sustainable livelihoods, and environmental stewardship, enabling resilience and equity for a brighter future."

We believe strong communities are the foundation for children to realize their aspirations — and that poverty is manmade and can be overcome.

What We Do

Our Programmes

Targeted interventions across seven key areas, reaching communities across Uganda.

📚

Education

  • Scholarships & bursaries from nursery through to university
  • School supplies for disadvantaged students
  • Teacher training in psychosocial skills
  • Building and renovating educational infrastructure
🏥

Health & Wellbeing

  • Healthcare services at BELL Foundation schools
  • Community health outreach programmes
  • Eye care & free sight lenses for students
  • Sanitation education through demonstrations
  • Support for girl child menstrual health
💧

Clean Water & Sanitation

  • Clean water sources and wells in schools & communities
  • Rainwater harvesting tanks for households
  • Pit latrine construction to improve sanitation
  • Sanitation education in schools and communities
🌾

Food Security & Emergency Aid

  • Emergency food aid during crises and disasters
  • Crop, fisheries & forestry farming for long-term food security
  • Organic, market-oriented farming education
  • Farming skills education in schools
💼

Livelihoods & Skills

  • Vocational training for employment & economic independence
  • Sustainable alternative livelihood activities
  • Computer & digital literacy in schools
  • Digital skills for out-of-school young people
🛡️

Community Disaster Resilience

  • Emergency response: food, water, shelter & medical supplies
  • Reconstruction & rehabilitation of damaged infrastructure
  • Psychosocial support and counselling for survivors
